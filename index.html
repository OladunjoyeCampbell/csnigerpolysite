About the Department

History: The Department of Computer Science at Niger State Polytechnic, Zungeru, began as a Computer Science Unit under the Department of Mathematics & Statistics. The unit initially offered certificate courses and later the National Diploma (ND) programme.

In 2008, the unit was upgraded to a full department. Mr. Oladele O. Campbell (now Dr. Oladele Campbell) became the pioneer Head of Department (2008–2012). Under his leadership, the department secured full accreditation for the Higher National Diploma (HND) in Computer Science, laying the foundation for advanced computing education.

The department’s leadership continued with:

Tenure Head of Department
2012–2016Dr. Ibrahim Umar Kontagora (now Rector of the Polytechnic)
2016–2020Mr. Isah Adamu Dagah
2020–2022Dr. Ahmed Isah Baba
2022–presentMr. Usman Ubandawaki (current HOD)

Since 1999, the department has graduated many sets of ND, HND, and PGD students. More recently, the first cohorts of the specialised HND tracks – Software & Web Development and Networking & Cloud Computing – have successfully graduated. Reaccreditation successes were recorded notably during the tenures of Dr. Ibrahim Kontagora and Mr. Isah Adamu Dagah.

Mission: To provide practical, industry-relevant computer science education fostering innovation, research, and entrepreneurial skills.

Vision: To be a centre of excellence in computing education, producing globally competitive and locally relevant graduates.
